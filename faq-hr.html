Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about entering and attending the Hack3r Runway contest.

How do I enter the contest?
Submit your entry using the registration form linked on the site. Prepare photos and a short description; bring your physical entry to DEF CON for the live showcase if selected.
What are the eligibility rules?
Entries must be handmade or custom-modified wearables. Store-bought-only items are not eligible. See the full rules on the submission form for details about materials and safety. We certainly welcome storebought items that have been significantly modified/hacked. Entries must be safe for public display and wear. No hazardous materials, weapons, or items that violate DEF CON's code of conduct. If in doubt, contact us before creating your entry.
What categories are available?
We accept Digital Wearable, Smart Wear, Aesthetics, and Functional Wear. Choose the category that best fits your entry when submitting. There will also be a People's Choice Award that is voted on by attendees.
When is the sign-up window?
Sign-up opens June 28, 2026 at 5PM PDT and closes August 8, 2026 at 12PM PDT. Times shown on the form are are subject to change if Defcon schedule is updated.
Do I need to be at DEF CON to participate?
Yes. All entries are expected to walk the runway or find a volunteer to showcase their creations. We are considering doing some online mini contests, but that will be for US participants only.
How are entries judged?
Judging criteria include, but are not limited to, creativity, uniqueness, craftsmanship, functionality, and alignment with category. Judges are selected volunteers from the community; decisions are final.
What are the submission requirements?
Pictures and/or videos showing your work progress and a description, category selection, social media handles, and contact info.
Who do I contact for questions?
Use the contact email on the registration form or DM @Hack3rRunway on Bluesky. For urgent on-site issues not related to the contest, event staff are the primary contact.
Can I submit more than one entry?
Yes, you can submit multiple entries. Each entry must be submitted separately with its own description and category selection.
Can I make something on site?
Yes. Past entries have brought along their materials and worked on site. There is also the Maker Village if you need workspace. Finally, this year, The Diana Initiative is offering a space for participants to create and collaborate on Friday morning at Defcon.
Can I volunteer to help with the contest?
Yes! We are always looking for volunteers to help with various aspects of the contest, including judging, event setup, and social media coverage. If you're interested in volunteering, please reach out via the contact email on the registration form or DM @Hack3rRunway on Bluesky. Remember it is strictly a volunteer position.
Will there be prizes?
Yes! We will have prizes for the top entries in each category, as well as a People's Choice Award. Prizes may include swag, gift cards, and other hacker-themed goodies. Stay tuned for more details on the prizes as we get closer to the event!
How can I sponsor the contest?
We welcome prizes sponsors who share our vision for promoting creativity and innovation in the hacker community. Sponsors will have to follow the Defcon guidelines for how to display their branding. Currently, the contest is sponsored by Hack3r Runway Shop.
